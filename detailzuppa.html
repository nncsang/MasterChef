ZUPPA

STEP 1


Cover the porcini with 250ml boiling water and leave to rehydrate for 20 minutes.

STEP 2


In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium-low heat and add the onion, carrots, celery and salt. Fry gently until the vegetables are very tender and beginning to caramelise – this should take 15-20 minutes. This flavour base is known as the soffritto, and it’s essential to fry it slowly so the sweetness of the vegetables is released.

STEP 3


Drain the porcini and reserve the soaking liquid. Coarsely chop the porcini and add to the pan along with the garlic and parsley. Fry for 5 minutes or until fragrant.

STEP 4


Add the tomatoes and break up gently with a wooden spoon. Cook for a few minutes then add the reserved porcini liquid and stock. Cook for another 15 minutes then add the beans and cook until heated through. For a thinner soup, add extra stock or water..

STEP 5


Taste and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Sprinkle with the extra parsley, drizzle with extra virgin olive oil and serve immediately with grilled bread.

  • 2 € 1 onion, coarsely chopped
  • 2 € 4 medium carrots, peeled, coarsely chopped
  • 2 € 2 celery sticks, coarsely chopped
  • 2 € 1 teaspoon sea salt
  • 2 € 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 2 € 1⁄2 cup flat-leafed parsley, finely chopped, plus extra to garnish
  • 2 € 1 x 400g can tomatoes
  • 2 € 500ml vegetable stock
  • 2 €1 x 400g can borlotti beans, drained, well rinsed
